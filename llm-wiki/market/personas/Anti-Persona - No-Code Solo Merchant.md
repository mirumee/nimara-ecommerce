## Content

### Who they are

A solo merchant looking for what Shopify sells: hosted storefront, theme editor, app store, no terminal required.

### Why excluded

Nimara is a self-hosted, composable, code-first stack. Serving no-code merchants would mean building hosting, theme editors, and one-click app installs — competing with Shopify on Shopify's terms while compromising the provider-swapping, code-ownership architecture that makes Nimara valuable to its actual adopters.

### How to handle them

When they ask "how do I install this without a terminal," the answer is a polite pointer to hosted alternatives, not a roadmap item.

### Note on end shoppers

End shoppers (buyers) are not an adoption segment — Nimara does not sell to them. They are documented as experience personas ([Shopper](Shopper.md) pre-purchase, [Customer](Customer.md) post-purchase) because storefront PRDs need them as a quality bar, but their experience remains the responsibility of the [Storefront Developer](Storefront%20Developer.md) and [Ecommerce Manager](Ecommerce%20Manager.md) personas. Shopper-facing issues (checkout, search, reviews, channels) should be treated as threats to the developer's trust and the manager's revenue.
