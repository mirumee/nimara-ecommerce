## Content

### Market size and direction
- The global modular/composable commerce market is projected to reach **$10.8B by 2035**, a **15.7% CAGR** from a **$2.5B** baseline in 2025.
- Growth is driven by larger merchants replacing legacy monolithic platforms to gain performance and multi-channel flexibility.
- The economic crossover favours **open-source, self-hosted** architectures once a brand passes roughly **$2M–$10M annual revenue** — above that point, hosted SaaS platform fees and GMV cuts become uneconomical, making owned, optimised infrastructure attractive.

### The adoption bottleneck
- Despite clear economics, **developer adoption is the competitive bottleneck**. Developers face extreme complexity wiring together decoupled services, producing "tutorial fatigue" and post-launch operational friction.
- This is exactly the gap Nimara's code-first, pre-integrated, deploy-fast positioning targets.

### Implication for Nimara
- Win the developer first (see [Storefront Developer](/product/personas/Storefront%20Developer.md)); the business operator ([Ecommerce Manager](/product/personas/Ecommerce%20Manager.md)) inherits the platform from them.
- Compete against gated enterprise platforms and complex self-hosted alternatives by staying fully open-source and removing setup friction.
