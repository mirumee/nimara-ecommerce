## Content

### Goals
- Find the right product quickly — by describing what they need, searching, or filtering — without learning the store's structure.
- Judge trustworthiness before paying: ratings, reviews, clear pricing, delivery expectations, and a store that feels professionally run.
- Get through checkout with minimum friction — ideally as a guest, on a phone, in under a minute.

### Pain Points
- Slow or janky pages, especially on mobile — they leave within seconds and never report why.
- Thin product pages: no reviews, no social proof, vague descriptions — no way to de-risk the purchase.
- Forced account creation, surprise costs at checkout, or unclear stock/delivery information.

### Behavior Patterns
- **Frequency:** Episodic and often first-time — no loyalty to the store yet, arriving from search, ads, or shared links.
- **Technical level:** Irrelevant — a consumer who expects things to simply work.
- **Decision process:** Compares every store to Amazon-grade UX regardless of the store's size; abandons silently at the first sign of friction or distrust.
- **Current solution:** Buys the same product wherever trust and convenience are higher — a marketplace or a competitor's store.

### Key Quote
> "No reviews, no stars, nothing? I'm not spending 200 zł to find out first."

### Product Implications
- Their experience is the [Storefront Developer](product/personas/Storefront%20Developer.md)'s trust and the [Ecommerce Manager](product/personas/Ecommerce%20Manager.md)'s revenue — shopper-facing gaps (performance, search, reviews display, checkout) are defects of the highest order, not enhancements.
- Social proof is a conversion mechanism: ratings and review counts must be visible on product cards and product pages by default (see the User Reviews epic).
- Discovery must serve both modes: describing a need in natural language and classic search/filter for shoppers who know what they want (see the LLM Product Discovery epic).
- Guest-first flows and mobile performance are defaults, not options — every barrier is measured in silent abandonment.
